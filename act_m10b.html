Activity 10B — BrightCore QA Implementation Plan

← Back to Course Hub
🎯 Learning Objective: Apply the full QA implementation framework to a new business context — designing a QA roadmap, stakeholder communication strategy, and phased rollout plan.

Scenario

BrightCore Consulting Group is a 65-person management consulting firm. They have no formal QMS in place. The Managing Director has asked you (newly appointed QA Lead) to build a QA function from scratch. Key context:
  • No SOPs exist — delivery is consultant-dependent
  • Client satisfaction data is collected but never reviewed systematically
  • Staff are skeptical — "we already do quality work, why do we need QA?"
  • The board wants external ISO 9001 certification within 18 months
  • Budget allocated: limited — one QA Lead (you), no QA team initially

Question 1 — Your 90-Day Roadmap

Design your first 90 days. What will you do in Month 1, Month 2, and Month 3? Be specific about deliverables and milestones.

Question 2 — Overcoming Staff Resistance

Staff are skeptical about QA. Write a short stakeholder communication message (suitable for an all-staff briefing email) that addresses their concern and builds buy-in.

Question 3 — ISO 9001 Readiness Timeline

The board wants certification in 18 months. Outline the major milestones from now to certification. What are the biggest risks to this timeline?

Model Guidance

190-Day Plan: Month 1 = assess and align (gap analysis, QA policy). Month 2 = build and document (SOPs, document register, awareness training). Month 3 = operate and report (first audit, KPI dashboard, board report). Milestones must be tangible deliverables, not activities.
2Staff Communication: Acknowledge their view, reframe QA as enablement not critique. Use "with you, not on you" language. Lead with practical benefits: repeatability, faster onboarding, stronger client trust.
318-Month Risks: Resistance, scope creep, resource limits, and audit findings requiring major rework are the four critical risk areas. Each needs a named mitigation. Certification timeline without leadership active sponsorship is the highest risk.
