Activity 7B — Quality KPI Dashboard Design

← Back to Course Hub
🎯 Learning Objective: Design a practical quality KPI dashboard for a business scenario, selecting appropriate metrics, targets, and data sources.

Scenario

ClearPath Staffing Solutions provides contract staff to 40 client businesses. Their quality manager has been asked to create a KPI dashboard to present to leadership monthly. The dashboard should give leadership a clear picture of: (1) service quality, (2) internal process efficiency, and (3) compliance posture.

Question 1 — Select Your 6 KPIs

Choose 6 KPIs for the dashboard (2 per category). For each, name the KPI, its target, and its data source.

Question 2 — Dashboard Layout Description

Describe how you would visually organize this dashboard for a leadership audience. What chart types and visual formats would you use and why?

Question 3 — Escalation Triggers

For two of your KPIs, define what threshold would trigger an escalation to senior management and what action would be required.

Model Guidance

1Strong KPIs are SMART: named, measurable, with a numeric target and named data source. Avoid vague KPIs like "improve quality." Each category needs at least one leading and one lagging indicator.
2Layout best practice: Traffic light summary at top, trend charts for trajectory KPIs, bar charts for period comparisons. Leadership needs at-a-glance status, not raw data tables.
3Escalation criteria should be pre-agreed thresholds — not reactive. Define both the trigger condition (e.g., below threshold for 2 consecutive periods) and the mandated response action (CAPA, review meeting, root cause investigation).
