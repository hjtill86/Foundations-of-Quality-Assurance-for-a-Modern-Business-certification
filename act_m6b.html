Activity 6B — NovaBake CAPA Case Study

← Back to Course Hub
🎯 Learning Objective: Apply root cause analysis tools to a real workplace nonconformance and develop a complete CAPA response.

Scenario

NovaBake Foods has received 47 customer complaints in the past 30 days about undercooked products. An internal review found:
  • Oven temperature sensor #3 was not calibrated in 8 months (last calibration due at 6 months)
  • The calibration schedule existed but was not being monitored by any assigned person
  • Operators were aware the oven "ran cold" but did not raise a nonconformance report (NCR)
  • No escalation path existed for operators to report suspected equipment deviations

Question 1 — Identify Root Causes (Fishbone Categories)

Using Fishbone (Ishikawa) categories — People, Method, Machine, Material, Measurement, Environment — classify each contributing factor and identify the deepest root cause.

Question 2 — Containment Action

What immediate containment actions should NovaBake take before the root cause is fully resolved? Name at least three.

Question 3 — Corrective and Preventive Actions

Write one corrective action (fix the identified root cause) and one preventive action (prevent recurrence across all ovens).

Question 4 — Verification of Effectiveness

How would you verify that your corrective action worked? Define a specific timeframe and measurable success criterion.

Model Answer

1Root Causes: Machine (overdue calibration), Method (unowned schedule), People (no escalation path). Deepest root cause: No system ownership for calibration monitoring — a process gap, not an individual error.
2Containment: (a) Hold all Oven #3 output pending inspection. (b) Suspend Oven #3 until recalibrated. (c) Notify customer service of potential impact. (d) Review last 30 days' production for other deviations.
3Corrective: Recalibrate Oven #3; assign named calibration owners per equipment line. Preventive: Implement a QMS-linked calibration due-date tracker with automated alerts and monthly management review.
4Verification: Review calibration log at 30, 60, 90 days. Pass criteria: 100% on-time calibration completion and zero customer complaints attributable to temperature deviation for 90 days.
