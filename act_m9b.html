Activity 9B — EliteLink Continuous Improvement Plan

← Back to Course Hub
🎯 Learning Objective: Apply the PDCA cycle to a real operational problem, write a structured NCR, and build a prioritized improvement backlog.

Scenario

EliteLink Communications manages call center operations for 15 client companies. Over the past quarter, their QA Manager has compiled the following data:
  • Average customer satisfaction score: 3.6 / 5.0 (target: 4.5)
  • First Call Resolution (FCR) rate: 61% (target: 80%)
  • Agent script adherence score: 54% (target: 90%)
  • NCR raised for script non-adherence — no corrective action assigned after 45 days
  • Last training refresh for all agents: 14 months ago (required every 6 months)

Question 1 — PDCA Improvement Plan

Using the PDCA framework, write a complete improvement plan to address the FCR and script adherence failures. Include at least two actions per phase.

Question 2 — Write a Formal NCR

The open NCR (raised 45 days ago with no action) is itself a nonconformance. Write a new escalation NCR for this process failure. Include: Description, Root Cause, Severity, and Required Action.

Question 3 — Improvement Backlog

List three items for EliteLink's improvement backlog. For each: name the improvement, the expected benefit, and the effort level (Low / Medium / High).

Model Answer

1PDCA: Plan — analyze call recordings, benchmark by agent. Do — refresher training, weekly monitoring. Check — track weekly FCR and CSAT at 30 and 60 days. Act — standardize coaching into SOP, schedule 6-monthly training refresh.
2Escalation NCR: NCR for unactioned CAPA is itself a Major NC. Root cause: no NCR ownership or escalation system. Required action: assign owner in 24 hrs, implement tracking, add to management review.
3Backlog: (1) NCR tracking dashboard — Medium effort, immediate compliance impact. (2) Agent training program rebuild — High effort, highest ROI. (3) Peer coaching — Low effort, fast skill transfer.
