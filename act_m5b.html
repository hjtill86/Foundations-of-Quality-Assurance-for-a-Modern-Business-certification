Activity 5B — Risk Control Evaluation Discussion

← Back to Course Hub
🎯 Learning Objective: Evaluate the adequacy of existing risk controls and propose improvements using the hierarchy of controls framework.

Scenario

Streamline Manufacturing produces plastic components. They have identified the following current controls for three key operational risks. Your task is to evaluate whether each control is sufficient and recommend improvements.

Risk A: Chemical spillage in the mixing area. Current control: Staff advised to "be careful" — verbal instruction only.

Risk B: Incorrect material loaded into press. Current control: Visual inspection by operator.

Risk C: Equipment failure causing production halt. Current control: Reactive maintenance — repair when broken.

Hierarchy of Controls Reference

1. Elimination 2. Substitution 3. Engineering Controls 4. Administrative Controls 5. PPE

Question 1 — Evaluate Risk A

Is "be careful" adequate as a control? What level of the hierarchy is this? What should replace or supplement it?

Question 2 — Evaluate Risk B

Assess the visual inspection control. What control level is this? What stronger control would reduce reliance on human judgment?

Question 3 — Evaluate Risk C

Reactive maintenance is not a risk control — it is a response. What proactive control should replace it? Name the control and explain its benefit.

Model Answer

ARisk A: "Be careful" is a weak administrative control. Replace with: (1) Engineering control — install spill containment bunds and chemical splash guards. (2) Administrative — formal chemical handling SOP and PPE requirement. (3) PPE — gloves, goggles, and apron mandated for all mixing tasks.
BRisk B: Visual inspection is an administrative control susceptible to fatigue and distraction. Stronger control: barcode scanning or RFID material verification at press loading point — an engineering control that prevents wrong material from being loaded regardless of operator attentiveness.
CRisk C: Reactive maintenance is not a control. Replace with a Preventive Maintenance Program: scheduled inspection and servicing at intervals based on manufacturer guidance and usage data. This eliminates unplanned breakdowns and converts risk management from reactive to proactive.
