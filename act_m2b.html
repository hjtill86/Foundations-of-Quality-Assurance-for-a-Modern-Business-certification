Activity 2B — Records Audit Readiness Reflection

← Back to Course Hub
🎯 Learning Objective: Identify the records required for a process to be audit-ready, assign ownership, and assess the consequences of missing records.

Reflection Prompt

Think about a process you are familiar with in your current or past workplace, or use the order fulfillment process from Activity 2A.

Question 1

What records would need to exist for this process to be considered audit-ready? List at least four specific record types.

Question 2

Who would be responsible for creating and maintaining each record type you identified?

Question 3

What would be the consequences of those records being missing or incomplete during an external audit?

Key Takeaways

✓Records must be accurate, complete, legible, and dated at the time they are created.
✓In QA: if it wasn't documented, it didn't happen — the auditor's standard.
✓Missing records = nonconformance, not an observation or opportunity for improvement.
✓Every step in a process should have a designated record owner with a defined retention period.
