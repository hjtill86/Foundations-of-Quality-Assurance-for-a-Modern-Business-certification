Activity 1B — Role Identification Case Study

🎯 Learning Objective: Identify which QA role failed in a real-world scenario and explain what each role should have done to prevent the situation.
📄 Submission Note: This is a written reflection activity. Type your response below. In a live course, your instructor will provide written feedback on your submission.

Scenario

A manufacturing company is preparing for its first external audit. The operations team is scrambling to locate current procedure documents. The production floor has been running an outdated SOP for three months, and customer complaints have risen 15% over the past quarter.


Senior leadership is unaware of the complaint trend. No internal audit has been conducted in over a year. When the external auditor arrives, the quality manager is unable to produce training records for six recently onboarded employees.

Your Task

1Identify which QA role(s) failed in this scenario: Process Owner, Quality Manager, or Internal Auditor.
2For each failed role, explain specifically what they should have done to prevent this situation.
3Your response should be 200–300 words, written in clear, professional language.

Your Response

Model Answer Guidance

After submitting, compare your response to the points below:

1Process Owner: Should have ensured the SOP on the production floor was the current approved version. Outdated documents indicate a failure to maintain controlled distribution and remove obsolete versions from use.
2Quality Manager: Should have monitored the customer complaint trend through regular KPI review and escalated to leadership. Should also have ensured training records were maintained for all new employees and a management review cadence was in place.
3Internal Auditor: Should have conducted internal audits at planned intervals, which would have identified the outdated SOP, missing records, and rising complaint trend before the external audit — giving the organization time to correct these gaps.
